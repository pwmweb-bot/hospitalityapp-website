What it is

Cellar checks app, defined.

A cellar checks app is mobile software that records a pub's daily, weekly and monthly cellar maintenance — line cleans, gas levels, cellar temperature, beer quality and stock rotation — with photo evidence and timestamps. It replaces the paper cellar logbook that breweries and pub operating companies typically require.

The cellar is the bit of the pub that quietly decides whether you have a pint worth selling. Get the checks right and beer quality stays consistent; get them wrong and even a great beer tastes off by Tuesday.

The checks

What goes in the log.

Daily — cellar temp

Logged morning and (ideally) afternoon. 11–13°C for cask, 3–7°C for keg lager. Drift over 2°C? Investigate before stock spoils.

Daily — gas levels

CO₂ and mixed-gas bottle levels. Photo of the gauge. Order before the changeover, not after the stockout.

Weekly — line clean

Beer line clean cycle. Cleaner concentration, contact time, rinse-water clarity, sample pull post-clean. Photo evidence required.

Daily — beer quality

First-pull check: clarity, foam retention, temperature, aroma. The pint that goes to the test customer is the pint that goes to every customer.

Weekly — stock rotation

Use-by dates per cask, keg connection dates, ullage log. Stock that's been on too long pours flat; the rotation log catches it.

Monthly — deep clean

Cellar floor and walls, coolers serviced, FOBs cleaned, drip trays sanitised. Photo evidence and engineer sign-off when applicable.

The brewery rep visit

Be ready before they walk in.

What the rep typically asks for.

Last four weeks of line-clean logs. Cellar temperature trend. Gas-changeover dates. Any quality complaints logged. If you're on a tied agreement, this is also the supplier compliance check — the rep is verifying the contract.

Why digital wins over paper.

Paper logs get water-damaged, lost, or "completed Sunday" on Monday morning. Digital with photo evidence and tamper-evident timestamps stops the rep wondering whether the log is real. Cleaner relationship; faster visit.

What an FOBI / spear-cleaning entry looks like.

Engineer visit date, parts replaced, photo of the work. Goes into the same log as the daily checks so the next rep sees the full maintenance history without asking.

How this protects beer quality complaints.

If a customer complains about a pint, the cellar log proves the line was clean and the temp was in band. The complaint becomes a single-pint issue (most likely a swap-tap, not a cellar problem) rather than a "your cellar is broken" issue.

FAQ

Cellar checks, asked & answered.

What is a cellar checks app?

Mobile software that records daily, weekly and monthly cellar maintenance with photo evidence — line cleans, gas, temperature, beer quality, stock rotation.

How often should beer lines be cleaned?

Weekly is the industry standard for most styles; cask ale often more frequent, lager sometimes acceptable at 14 days with the right chemistry.

What temperature should a beer cellar be?

11–13°C for cask ale, 3–7°C for keg lager. Steady is more important than exact.

Does the brewery actually look at the log?

Yes — most pub operating companies and breweries require it under tied-house contracts and spot-check on visits.
