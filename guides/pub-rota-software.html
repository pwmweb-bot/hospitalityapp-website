Pub rota software, in plain English.

What an actually-useful pub rota app does — and the seven warning signs that the tool your pub is using isn't pulling its weight. Written by people who've run the Saturday rota themselves.

What it is

Pub rota software, defined.

Pub rota software is a scheduling app built for the specific shape of pub work — role-aware shifts (bar, kitchen, door, glass collector), swap requests, sports-event templates, holiday tracking and labour-cost % live against takings. The best UK options are also licensing-aware: storing personal-licence holders, BIIAB qualifications and refusal logs against the same staff records.

What it isn't: a generic timesheet tool. A retail-shift app. A spreadsheet with a pretty front-end. Pubs have rhythms — sports fixtures, late-night closing, cellar deliveries — and the tool that ignores them creates more work than it saves.

The checklist

The seven features pub rota software needs.

If your current tool is missing three or more of these, you're paying for software that doesn't know it's in a pub.

1 · Role chips

Bartender, kitchen, door, glass collector, duty manager. Shifts are typed; staff are typed; the rota only assigns matches.

2 · Sports-event templates

"Six Nations Saturday" template — double-handed bar, glass, door. Built once, dropped on every fixture day for the season.

3 · Swap requests

Staff swap among themselves, eligible by role. Manager approves with one tap; rota auto-updates. Kills the WhatsApp chaos.

4 · Licensing-aware staff

Personal licence + BIIAB qualifications stored against the staff record. Renewal-tracked alerts before any licence lapses.

5 · Labour-cost % live

Today's takings → today's labour cost → the band tells you whether to send someone home an hour early.

6 · Photo-evidenced clock-in

Optional clock-in selfie or PIN to stop buddy-punching. Honest hours, honest payroll.

7 · Mobile-first for staff

Bar staff don't open laptops. The phone app is where the rota gets viewed, swap-requested and clocked into. Web is for managers.

Warning signs

If your pub rota tool does any of this, replace it.

It charges per staff member.

Per-user pricing penalises seasonal hiring. Pub rota software should price per venue, not per head — otherwise your busy summer costs more than your dead January.

It can't store a personal licence.

If the licensing officer turns up and your rota tool can't tell them who the DPS is, the tool wasn't built for pubs.

Swap requests go through email.

Bar staff don't open emails. Swaps need to be in-app, in-thread, approval-on-tap.

There's no role concept.

If you can schedule a glass collector as a chef, your rota will, eventually, schedule a glass collector as a chef. Roles aren't a nice-to-have.

It exports to PDF only.

The chef needs the kitchen rota; the duty manager needs everyone. Filtered, in-app views beat a 14-page printout every time.
