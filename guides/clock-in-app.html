What it is

Clock-in app, defined.

A clock-in app is software that records when staff start and finish each shift, replacing paper timesheets and spreadsheet-based hour tracking. For hospitality, the best clock-in apps run on a shared tablet behind the bar or in the kitchen — the bartender taps their face, hits clock in, gets back to pouring. At the end of the week the recorded hours feed straight into the payroll export, no re-keying.

The reason it matters: even a five-person team running on paper timesheets loses a few hours a week to estimation, illegible handwriting and the staff member who "forgot" to write down their start time. Over a year that's the difference between a profitable Q1 and a "we'll catch up in summer" Q1.

The six features

What a hospitality clock-in app should do.

1 · Multiple verification methods

Photo, PIN, QR code on the rota, NFC tag at the till. Different venues lean different ways; one tool covers all four so you don't pick a tech you'll outgrow.

2 · Geo-fence

Optional GPS perimeter — clock-in only works inside the venue postcode. Stops the "I clocked in from home" claim before it lands.

3 · Anti buddy-punching

Photo selfie at clock-in, visible in the duty manager's daily review. One spot-check finds the friend who's been clocking in their flatmate.

4 · Forgotten clock-out catcher

Shift open past scheduled end +30min? Manager gets a notification. Edit from the inbox, audit trail records the edit.

5 · Hours feed payroll directly

Clock-in/out timestamps × hourly rate = wages. Labour-cost % calculated against today's takings. CSV export ready for your payroll provider.

6 · Mobile or shared tablet

Staff use their own phone, or share a venue tablet near the door. The shared-tablet flow is the one that actually gets used in busy bars.

Buddy-punching, ranked

Four ways to stop the friend-of-a-friend clock-in.

In strength order. Most venues run two or three of these in combination — the photo + geo-fence pair is enough for almost everyone.

1 · PIN

Each staff member has a 4-digit PIN. Quick, cheap, but shareable — a determined buddy-puncher just asks for the PIN. Useful as a baseline; not a real defence on its own.

2 · Photo selfie at clock-in

The phone or tablet camera takes a still at the moment of clock-in. Manager reviews the gallery each morning. A single spot-check catches the obvious cases; the deterrent effect kills the rest.

3 · Geo-fence (GPS perimeter)

Clock-in only works inside a defined radius of the venue postcode. The "I clocked in from home" defence collapses. Pairs naturally with photo selfie — geo-fence stops remote clock-in, selfie stops the in-venue friend.

4 · NFC tag

Each staff member gets a physical NFC tag (key fob or card). Tap to clock in or out. Highest friction to fake — has to physically be at the venue, has to physically hold the tag. Overkill for most pubs; standard for larger hotels.

FAQ

Clock-in apps, asked & answered.

What is a clock-in app for staff?

Software that records when staff start and finish each shift, replacing paper timesheets. The best hospitality clock-in apps run on a shared tablet behind the bar and feed hours straight into payroll.

How do you stop buddy-punching?

Photo selfie at clock-in (manager spot-checks), PIN (baseline), geo-fence (GPS perimeter), NFC tag (highest friction). Two or three combined defeats almost all attempts.

Is a digital clock-in legal in the UK?

Yes. Working Time Regulations 1998 require adequate records, but don't mandate paper. Digital with tamper-evident timestamps is widely accepted by HMRC and ACAS.

How does the clock-in app feed payroll?

Timestamps × hourly rate = wages. CSV export ready for any UK payroll provider. Labour-cost % calculated live against takings.

What if a staff member forgets to clock out?

Auto-flagged 30 minutes after scheduled shift end; manager edits from the inbox, edit logged with who and when.
