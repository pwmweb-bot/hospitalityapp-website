What it is

Hospitality POS, defined.

A hospitality POS — point of sale — is the software bar and waiting staff use to take orders, send them to the kitchen or bar, take payment and record the sale. Some people call it an EPOS (electronic point of sale), some call it a till app. Same thing.

What makes a hospitality POS different from a generic retail till is the rhythm of the work it has to handle: drinks-led menus with half-pint and pint pricing, course management for restaurants, allergen prompts at the moment of order, split-bill payments, table service, refusal logs for licensed premises. A till built for clothing retail won't survive a Saturday night in a busy pub.

The eight features

What a hospitality POS should do.

If your current till is missing three or more of these, it wasn't built for the job.

1 · Drinks-led menu pages

Categories that match a bar's brain — Draught, Bottles & Cans, Wine, Spirits, Cocktails, Soft & Mixers. Half and pint pricing on one card, not buried in a sub-menu.

2 · Course management

For restaurants — starters fire to the pass, mains hold, desserts on demand. Kitchen sees what's ordered and what's pending.

3 · Allergen prompts at the till

Server taps an item that contains nuts, the till prompts: "Has the guest confirmed no allergy?" Natasha's-Law ready, server-trained-and-logged.

4 · Split bills, the easy way

Split evenly, split by item, split by seat. Bar staff don't open a calculator. Service flows; tips don't get awkward.

5 · Refusal log access

Bar staff hit "Refused — under-25 no ID" without leaving the till. Licensing log builds itself; test-purchase season stays calm.

6 · Card terminal agnostic

Works with Stripe, SumUp, Square, Zettle or your existing standalone terminal. The till records the sale; the processor takes the card.

7 · Discount + note flow

Staff discount, NHS rate, comp drinks — applied at line or whole-bill level, logged with reason, exportable for the owner's review.

8 · Takings sync with payroll

Every sale feeds the daily takings total, which feeds the labour-cost %, which feeds the payroll export. No re-keying, no end-of-week reconciliation.

By venue type

The same till engine, three different modes.

Setup picks your venue type; the till's layout, default menu structure and compliance prompts change automatically.

Pub mode — drinks-led, fast counter service

Default landing page is Draught. Half + pint pricing on the same card. Cellar checks accessible from a side button. Refusal log one tap away. Sports-event "second-screen" mode shows tab management for crowded fixtures. The Crown & Anchor screenshot above is the till in pub mode.

Restaurant mode — course management, table service

Default landing is the dining-room table map. Courses fire in waves (starter → main → dessert) with kitchen printer routing. Allergen prompt is mandatory before the order sends. Split-bill flow is built for the "we'll split the wine but I had the steak" reality.

Café mode — fast counter, modifier-heavy menu

Default landing is Coffee + Pastries. Modifiers (oat milk, extra shot, decaf) chain in three taps. Fast tab management for the morning rush. PPDS labels print at the till if a label printer is connected.

Bar mode — cocktails, late-night, door staff

Default landing is Cocktails. Spec-card per cocktail visible to staff (recipe + glassware). Pre-mix vs made-to-order flagged. Door-staff handover at close-time runs through the till. Late-night refreshment evidence pack builds itself.

FAQ

Hospitality POS, asked & answered.

What is a hospitality POS?

Software for taking orders, sending them to the kitchen or bar, taking payment and recording the sale — built for the rhythms of pubs, restaurants and cafés (drinks-led menus, course management, allergens, split bills, refusal logs).

How much does a hospitality POS cost in the UK?

Typically £30–£90 per till per month for software, £400–£1,200 per terminal for hardware (or £15–£40 per month leased), plus 1.0–1.7% card-processing fees. HospitalityApp includes the till on the Complete plan at £149/venue/mo, or as a £59 add-on to Professional (£99/mo). No per-terminal fees either way.

Do I need a separate EPOS or can my rota app run the till?

All-in-one platforms like HospitalityApp run the till inside the same app as rota, compliance and payroll. Staff records, allergen matrices and labour-cost calculations stay in sync without an integration layer.

Does a pub need a different POS to a restaurant?

Same engine, different modes. Pub mode leads with Draught and half/pint pricing; restaurant mode leads with course management and allergen sign-off at the till; café mode leads with modifier-heavy fast service.

Can the POS integrate with my existing payment processor?

Yes — HospitalityApp's till is processor-agnostic. Works with Stripe, SumUp, Square, Zettle (where allowed) and standalone terminals. Keep your existing card machine and contract.

Does the till handle Natasha's Law allergen labelling?

The till holds the allergen matrix per menu item and prompts the server before order send. For PPDS labelling it drives a label printer or hands off to your existing labelling system. Allergen data lives in one place.
