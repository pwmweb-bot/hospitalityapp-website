What it is

HACCP app, defined.

A HACCP app is mobile software that digitises a kitchen's Hazard Analysis Critical Control Point records — temperature logs, cleaning schedules, supplier checks and corrective actions — with photo evidence, timestamps and pass/fail enforcement at log time. It replaces the paper diary that the FSA's Safer Food Better Business pack provides.

It is not a hazard analysis tool. The hazard analysis (the "HA" of HACCP) is a thinking exercise that your business does once and reviews when the menu or process changes. The app handles the daily monitoring, verification and record-keeping that flow from that analysis.

The seven principles

HACCP, principle by principle.

The app's job is to make principles 4–7 happen automatically. You still own principles 1–3 — they're the strategy that shapes what the app logs.

1 · Hazard analysis

Identify biological, chemical and physical hazards in your processes. Owner exercise; review when menu changes.

2 · Critical control points

Decide where in your process you control each hazard (cooking, cooling, cold storage, hot hold).

3 · Critical limits

The numbers: 75°C core for cooked meat, 5°C for chilled, 63°C for hot hold. Set once.

4 · Monitoring

App-driven: temperature logs at intervals, with min/max bands enforced at log time.

5 · Corrective actions

When a check fails, the app prompts the action (discard, recook, repair, recall) and records it.

6 · Verification

Manager sign-off on entries; periodic audits; calibration logs for probes and fridges.

7 · Record-keeping

Photo-evidenced entries, timestamps, tamper-evident storage. Exportable PDF for the inspector.

What a HACCP app must do

Six features that separate an app from a digital clipboard.

1 · Pass/fail at log time

The probe reads 12°C and the fridge band is 0–5°C. The app marks the entry FAIL at log time and prompts a corrective action. A digital clipboard would log the number and let the manager find out at month-end.

2 · Photo evidence required

For high-risk checks (cooked-food core temp, cleaning sign-off after a deep clean) the app blocks submission until a photo is attached. Stops "ticking through" without doing.

3 · Tamper-evident timestamps

Server-side timestamps, not device-side. The kitchen can't backdate a missed Sunday log to Monday — the inspector sees when it was actually logged.

4 · Role-aware sign-off

Staff log; managers verify. The same person can't both record and sign off — that's where audit trails fail.

5 · Corrective action workflow

Failed check → prompted action → action recorded → manager notified. The audit trail proves the kitchen acted on the failure, not just noted it.

6 · One-tap export

EHO at the door? Tap export, share the PDF of the last 12 months. Not a binder hunt; not a frantic Excel reformat.

FAQ

HACCP apps, asked & answered.

What is a HACCP app?

Mobile software that digitises Hazard Analysis Critical Control Point records — temperature logs, cleaning, supplier checks, corrective actions — with photo evidence and pass/fail enforcement.

Is it accepted by the EHO?

Yes. UK EHOs accept digital food-safety records that are tamper-evident, exportable on request and cover the same scope as a paper diary.

Does HACCP apply to small cafés and B&Bs?

Yes — Article 5 of EC 852/2004 (retained) requires all food business operators to implement HACCP-based procedures, regardless of size.

What are the seven HACCP principles?

1) Hazard analysis. 2) Critical control points. 3) Critical limits. 4) Monitoring. 5) Corrective actions. 6) Verification. 7) Record-keeping.
