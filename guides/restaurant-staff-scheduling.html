What it is

Restaurant staff scheduling, defined.

Restaurant staff scheduling is the practice of building shift rotas that match the kitchen brigade and front-of-house team to forecast covers, with role-aware templates, swap-request workflows and live labour-cost percentage as takings come in. Done well, it keeps payroll inside band without leaving the chef short on a Saturday.

Done badly, it's three WhatsApp groups, a Sunday-night spreadsheet, and a manager who finds out about the no-show 20 minutes before service.

The principles

Six rules that make restaurant scheduling work.

1 · One rota, all roles

Kitchen + FOH on one screen. Role chips — KP, CDP, sous, head chef, server, host, runner — assigned to shifts.

2 · Templates beat blanks

"Friday dinner" with the standard brigade saved once, dropped on every Friday. Edit the exceptions, not the whole thing.

3 · Schedule against covers

Last Friday's covers + this Friday's bookings + the weather = your starting point. Not last Friday alone.

4 · Swap inbox, not group chat

Staff post swaps, role-matched colleagues see them, manager approves with one tap. The chat thread disappears.

5 · Labour-cost % live

Today's labour cost calculated from today's rota and yesterday's takings. If it's drifting, you spot it at 3pm, not at month-end.

6 · Training on the staff record

Allergen-trained, level 2 food handler, manual handling — all sit on the staff record. Schedule the trained, not the available.

The trap

Where restaurant scheduling breaks down.

"I'll just copy last week."

Last week's rota included Tuesday off for half the kitchen because of a wedding. Copy without checking and you'll be short a CDP next Tuesday. Templates by service-day beat copy-paste-by-week every time.

"WhatsApp's fine for swaps."

WhatsApp's fine until two people swap into the same shift and nobody told the duty manager. Swap inbox with approval workflow stops the double-book at the source.

"Labour cost is a payroll problem."

Labour cost is a Tuesday afternoon problem. By Friday, it's structural. By month-end, it's the difference between profit and loss. Live tracking is the only way to manage it.

"My chef does the kitchen rota separately."

Then your chef's rota doesn't see the swap requests from FOH and your FOH rota doesn't see when the kitchen calls in a third KP. One rota, role chips. The chef still owns the kitchen line — they just write it in the same place.

FAQ

Restaurant scheduling, asked & answered.

What is restaurant staff scheduling?

The practice of building shift rotas that match the brigade and FOH team to forecast covers, with role-aware templates, swap workflows and live labour-cost %.

What's a good labour-cost % for a UK restaurant?

Most independents target 25–32% labour cost vs net sales. The number that matters is the trend against your own previous quarter, not an industry benchmark.

Do you need different tools for kitchen and FOH?

No. One rota with role chips covers both. Splitting tools doubles admin and breaks swap workflows.

How do you handle tronc?

Tronc is handled by your payroll provider on top of the rota's timesheet. HospitalityApp exports the timesheet CSV ready for any UK payroll provider.
