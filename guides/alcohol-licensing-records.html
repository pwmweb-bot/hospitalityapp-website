What you need on file

The six licensing records every venue keeps.

This is what the licensing officer asks for, every visit. Get these tidy and the visit is a chat, not a hearing.

1 · Premises licence + summary

The licence document plus the public-facing summary. Both on display at the venue; both on file (photo or scan) for off-site reference.

2 · Challenge 25 / Challenge 21 policy

Written, signed by the DPS, accessible to staff. Reviewed annually. The policy is what backs up the refusal log when the licensing officer asks.

3 · Refusal log

Every refused sale: date, time, reason, ID type checked, staff member. The log no licensing officer skips.

4 · DPS notice

The named DPS, their personal-licence number, contact details. On the premises, on file, on the wall.

5 · Personal-licence register

Every staff member authorised to make sales. Personal-licence number, issuing authority, renewal date.

6 · Age-verification training records

Per-staff training log: Challenge 25 briefed, ID types accepted, role-play test passed. The proof staff are competent.

The test purchase

What happens when the test arrives.

Trading Standards or the local-authority licensing team send an under-18 (or under-25) into your venue to attempt an alcohol purchase. The visit is recorded. If the sale is made, you face a review, a fine and a potential summary review of your licence. Here's how to be ready.

The visit, step by step.

Test purchaser enters. Approaches the bar. Orders alcohol. Staff member greets. If staff member challenges (asks for ID), refuses sale, logs refusal — the visit is a pass. If staff member sells, the sale is recorded, the purchaser leaves, and the licensing team writes within days.

What the licensing officer asks for next.

Your refusal log (the last six months minimum). Your Challenge 25 policy. Your training records for the staff member who made the sale (if you failed) or the staff member who refused (if you passed — they often praise good practice openly).

What "Section 182" guidance says about due diligence.

Statutory guidance under the Licensing Act 2003 expects a "due diligence" defence: a written policy, training records, a refusal log and supervision. All four matter — none alone is enough.

Why the log matters even when you've never refused.

A blank refusal log reads as "we never check ID". A populated one — even with three refusals a month — reads as a vigilant venue. Quiet pubs in safe areas still need to see entries.

FAQ

Alcohol licensing records, asked & answered.

What records do UK pubs need to keep?

Premises licence + summary, Challenge 25/21 policy, refusal log, DPS notice, personal-licence register, age-verification training records.

What's a refusal log?

A record of every refused alcohol sale — date, time, reason, ID type, staff member. Licensing officers ask for it on every test-purchase visit.

Who is the DPS?

The Designated Premises Supervisor — the named personal-licence holder responsible for authorising every alcohol sale at the venue.

Is Challenge 25 required by law?

The policy itself isn't statutory; verifying age is. Challenge 25 is the industry standard because it gives staff a margin of safety.
