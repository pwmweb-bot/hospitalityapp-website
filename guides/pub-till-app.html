What it is

Pub till app, defined.

A pub till app is point-of-sale software designed for the rhythms of pub work — a drinks-led menu with half-pint and pint pricing on the same card, fast counter service, cellar-check access from the till, a refusal log a tap away, and a sports-event mode that flips the layout for fixture days. It runs on an iPad or Android tablet behind the bar, replacing legacy EPOS terminals that were built for retail and grafted onto hospitality afterwards.

The difference matters. A retail till with a beer skin doesn't know that a half is half the price of a pint, that Cask Marque audits expect a cellar log, that Friday-night service collapses if the second bartender has to navigate three menus to pour a round.

The seven features

What a pub till should do.

Use this as the checklist when you're shopping. Anything missing three or more wasn't built for the trade.

1 · Half + pint on one card

The fundamental pub-till test. Both prices visible, both tappable, both feeding the same product code. Not a sub-menu, not a modifier, not a separate SKU.

2 · Drinks-led category order

Draught first. Bottles & cans second. Wine, spirits, cocktails, mixers, then food. Match the bar's brain; staff find what they need in under a second.

3 · Refusal log from the till

Bar staff refuse a sale, tap a "Refused" button on the till, log reason + ID type. Entry feeds the alcohol licensing file. Test-purchase season looks after itself.

4 · Cellar checks one tap away

Daily line-clean, gas level, cellar temp — accessible from the till's side button. The brewery rep walks in, you tap export, hand them the PDF.

5 · Sports-event mode

Six Nations Saturday flips the layout — bigger touch targets, tab strip for grouped rounds, faster categories. The Champions League final isn't the night to discover your till's limits.

6 · Card-processor agnostic

Use Stripe, SumUp, Square, Zettle or your existing standalone terminal. The till records the sale; your card machine takes the card. Keep your existing rate.

7 · Takings feed labour cost

Every pint sold updates today's takings, which updates today's labour-cost %. Spot the Wednesday wobble at 3pm, not at month-end.

A Friday night, scripted

What service looks like on the till.

17:00 · Pre-shift open

Duty manager opens the till. Daily compliance prompts surface: fridge temps, cellar gas, line clean if due. Each photographed, each logged. Cash float counted, recorded against opening reading. Bar staff clock in via the till using PIN or face.

19:30 · Two-deep at the bar

Half of Guinness, pint of Neck Oil — three taps. The half/pint card means no fumbling. Card payment via SumUp terminal connected over Bluetooth. Total updates the night's takings in real time. Bartender moves to the next customer.

21:00 · ID check, then refused sale

Customer orders. Bartender asks for ID. ID looks off. Bartender taps "Refused" on the till — picks "Suspected fake ID", time-stamped, brief note, optional photo of the bottle they were going to pour. Entry lands in the licensing file. Customer leaves; bartender moves on.

23:00 · Last orders, then close

Tab strip across the top shows open tabs. Bartender closes each one. Final Z-read run from the till. Takings figure auto-populates today's total. Closing checklist runs through the till: kitchen off, music off, bins out, alarm armed — each photographed.

What a tied pub pays vs an independent.

Brewery-supplied EPOS

Often "free" with a tied agreement. Real cost: locked into the brewery's stocklist, the brewery's card processor, the brewery's reporting schedule. Renegotiating the tie means migrating tills.

Standalone pub EPOS

£45–£89/mo software + £400–£1,200 per terminal + integration fees. Decent if you have three tills and a head office. Heavy overhead for a single-site free house.
