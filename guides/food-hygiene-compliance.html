What it is

Food hygiene compliance, defined.

UK food hygiene compliance is the set of legal duties under the Food Safety Act 1990 and Regulation (EC) 852/2004 (retained in UK law) that any food business must meet, plus the documented evidence that proves you're meeting them. For most independent hospitality operators it's enforced via local-authority Environmental Health Officer inspections and scored under the Food Hygiene Rating Scheme (FHRS), 0 to 5.

The shortcut: you have to do the right things and be able to show you've done the right things. Both. Doing without recording is a 2 or 3. Recording without doing is the kind of thing that ends careers.

The categories

The six things you need on file.

This is what the EHO opens first, every time. Get these tidy and the rest of the inspection feels like a conversation, not a cross-examination.

1 · Temperature logs

Fridges, freezers, hot-hold, cooked-food core temps. Daily, with min/max bands and pass/fail at log time.

2 · Cleaning schedule

Daily, weekly, monthly tasks — what, who, when. Signed off when done; photo-evidenced on deep cleans.

3 · Allergen records

Menu allergen matrix, supplier ingredient list, server training sign-off per menu change. Natasha's Law evidence.

4 · Supplier list

Approved suppliers with delivery checks (temperature, packaging, use-by). Traceability one step back, one step forward.

5 · Fitness to work

Staff sickness/return-to-work declarations (48-hour rule for vomiting/diarrhoea). Simple log, photo-signature.

6 · Training records

Level 2 food hygiene per staff handling food, allergen training, plus any role-specific (HACCP for management).

FHRS scoring

What gets you to a 5.

The EHO scores three areas; each can drop you a band. To get to FHRS-5 you need to score well in all three — a perfect kitchen with sloppy records is still a 3.

Hygienic food handling

Preparation, cooking, cooling, storage. Cross-contamination controls, temperature controls during prep, time-temp combinations for cooking and reheating. Watched in real time during the visit and inferred from your records.

Structural condition

Cleanliness, layout, lighting, ventilation, pest control evidence, drainage, hand-wash basins, surfaces. Mostly visible on the day — but your cleaning schedule and pest log back it up.

Confidence in management

Your documented food-safety management system. HACCP or SFBB. Daily diary, fitness-to-work declarations, supplier checks, training records. This is where most independents lose points — not because they aren't doing the work, but because they can't prove it.

FAQ

Food hygiene compliance, asked & answered.

What is food hygiene compliance in the UK?

The legal duties under the Food Safety Act 1990 and EC 852/2004 (retained), plus the documented evidence that proves you're meeting them. Enforced via EHO inspections, scored under FHRS 0–5.

What does an EHO inspector look for?

Three areas: hygienic food handling, structural condition, and confidence in management (your documented system — usually HACCP-based or SFBB).

Do I need a written HACCP plan?

Yes — Article 5 of EC 852/2004 requires a permanent procedure based on HACCP principles. Small operators usually satisfy this via the FSA's SFBB pack; larger operations need a documented HACCP plan.

How long should records be kept?

No single statutory period for hospitality, but 12 months minimum is the practical standard. Many operators keep three years; digital with photo evidence is widely accepted.
