What it is

Hospitality rota app, defined.

A hospitality rota app is mobile-first scheduling software built for pubs, restaurants, cafés, bars, hotels and B&Bs. It handles role-aware shift assignment, swap requests, holiday tracking and labour-cost percentage — and the best ones tie into the same venue's compliance records, training data and payroll export.

It's not a calendar. It's not a general workforce-scheduling tool. It's not a spreadsheet that learned to send push notifications. The shape of the work — split shifts, last-minute swaps, role-typed staff, event-driven templates — is specific to hospitality, and a tool that ignores that creates more work than it saves.

The checklist

Eight things a hospitality rota app must do.

1 · Price per venue

Not per user. Per-user pricing punishes seasonal hiring and high-turnover teams (i.e. everyone in hospitality).

2 · Role chips on staff

Bartender, chef, host, housekeeper. The shift knows what role it needs; the staff record knows what roles they can fill.

3 · Shift templates

"Saturday dinner", "Sunday roast", "Six Nations Sat" — built once, dropped on every matching day.

4 · Swap inbox

Staff swap among themselves, manager approves with one tap. WhatsApp chaos eliminated.

5 · Mobile-first for staff

The phone app is where the rota gets viewed and swap-requested. Web is for managers.

6 · Labour cost % live

Today's rota + yesterday's takings = today's labour %. Spot the wobble before payroll closes.

7 · Compliance baked in

Training records, allergen sign-offs, licence renewals all sit on the same staff records the rota uses.

8 · Payroll export

Timesheet CSV ready for any UK payroll provider. No re-keying, no copy-paste from a PDF.

The pricing trap

Per-user pricing is a tax on growth.

Independent hospitality runs hot. Summer staff, Christmas staff, leavers, the new starter who lasts two weeks. If your rota app charges per user, every one of those costs you. Per-venue pricing flattens it: one number, predictable, scales with success not headcount.

"But our team only has 12 people, so per-user is cheaper."

Today, yes. The moment you hire seasonal cover or open a second site, the bill jumps. Per-venue pricing tracks the value you get from the tool, not the cost of having staff.

"What about pay-per-shift pricing?"

Same problem in different clothes. Busy weeks are exactly when you need the tool to feel cheap.

"How do per-venue plans handle multi-site?"

Multi-site pricing per venue. HospitalityApp's Multi-site plan adds central admin across two or more venues without changing the per-venue rate.

FAQ

Hospitality rota apps, asked & answered.

What is a hospitality rota app?

Mobile-first scheduling software built for hospitality venues. Role-aware shifts, swap workflows, labour-cost %, plus compliance and payroll tie-in.

What's the difference vs general scheduling?

General tools miss split shifts, role chips, event-day templates, licensing data, swap workflows and live labour-cost — all of which a hospitality rota app handles out of the box.

How much should one cost?

UK independents typically pay £30–£90 per venue per month. Avoid per-user pricing.

Should it include compliance?

Yes — staff records used for the rota are the same ones used for training, licences and sign-offs. Splitting them creates drift.
